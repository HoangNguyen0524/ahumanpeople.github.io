The Zika Virus

Introduction Background Prototype Contact

Zika is a disease which is spread through mosquito bites. The symptoms caused by the Zika virus are mild and usually last less than a week. Some of the symptoms include pain in the back of eyes, joints and muscles. The host may feel fatigue, chills, fever, loss of appetite, and sweating. Headaches, skin rashes, vomiting, and eye redness are also common to have with Zika. So far there are no specific vaccines or treatments for Zika. Although there are no specific treatments for Zika, there are ways to help prevent you from obtaining it. Some of these methods include bug spray, bed nets, and long sleeve clothes. Mosquitos thrive in moist, damp, hot areas rather than cold areas therefore Zika mostly affects people in warmer climates. Zika is especially dangerous for pregnant women because Zika can cause birth defects for the unborn child.
